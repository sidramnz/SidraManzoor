About me

Domestic Helper Training

A case study on the analysis of training sessions focussing on teaching domestic help tasks and professional etiquette to newly recruited illiterate employees.

Individual Project

Location: Mauqa Online, Islamabad

Time: 3 weeks


Background

Mauqa Online is a domestic service company that provides illiterate workers with domestic help jobs. These helpers are trained by Mauqa and sent to customer homes to provide services like cooking, cleaning, babysitting and laundry.

Training sessions include tips and instructions on how to effectively complete domestic help tasks, behave professionally with customers and carry out the logistics of completing a job. Unfortunately, receiving numerous customer complaints about unprofessional or untrained helpers and getting feedback from helpers about confusion about training material prompted us to find ways to improve the training sessions.


Problem Statement

How might we improve the helper training process?


Research & Data Collection

In order to get a direct understanding of training and its possible issues, I decided to observe 4 training sessions and take notes without directly interfering with the sessions.

After gaining insights from observations, I conducted a focus group with helpers to understand their perspective about training sessions. I then synthesized our research to uncover key issues.


Ideation

I proposed the following action plan to solve the cash deposit issue:

  • To make the training powerpoint easier to comprehend, use primarily images, symbols and videos instead of text. And if needed, to use only Urdu text over English because majority of the helpers could not read English and some could also not read Urdu.
  • To encourage helper participation, the trainer should always call on helpers by their names when asking questions because I found that helpers were more active if the trainer had used their name. Also, the trainer should continuously ask helpers questions and to repeat concepts while the session is in order to gauge the helpers’ attention level.
  • To limit distractions during training, helpers should be told to keep phones on silent during the training session. Moreover, there should be a “Training in session” sign on the training room door to ensure that people from outside don’t enter the room and distract the helpers while training is in session.
